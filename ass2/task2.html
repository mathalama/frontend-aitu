Walter White

About Walter White

Walter Hartwell White Sr., also known by his clandestine alias Heisenberg, is the main protagonist of the American crime drama television series Breaking Bad. The character was created by Vince Gilligan and is portrayed by Bryan Cranston. Walter is a high school chemistry teacher turned methamphetamine manufacturer. He adopts the alias "Heisenberg" to conceal his identity while engaging in illegal activities. Walter's transformation from a mild-mannered teacher to a ruthless drug lord is a central theme of the series. His character is complex, driven by a mix of desperation, pride, and a desire to provide for his family after being diagnosed with terminal lung cancer. Walter White is widely regarded as one of the greatest and most iconic characters in television history.

Key Characteristics:

  • Intelligent and resourceful.
  • Determined and ambitious.
  • Morally complex and often ruthless.
  • Protective of his family.

Inventions & Contributions:

  • High-quality methamphetamine ("Blue Sky").
  • Application of chemistry to practical problems.
  • Contributions to science (in a twisted way).
